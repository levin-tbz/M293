Rösti

There is nothing more comforting than a perfectly executed Swiss Rösti, the ultimate mountain comfort food. This recipe transforms humble potatoes into a golden-brown masterpiece that is crispy on the outside and tender within. Whether served as a hearty breakfast or a side for Zürcher Geschnetzeltes, it’s a masterclass in how simple ingredients can create complex, satisfying textures.

Dauer: 45 Minutes

Personen: 2-3 People

Zutat Menge Einheit
Firm-cooking potatoes (parboiled) 800 Grams
Clarified butter (Ghee) 3 Tablespoons
Fine Sea Salt 1 Teaspoon
Cold Water 2 Tablespoons
Fresh Chives (optional) 1 Small bunch

Zubereitung

  1. Grate the Potatoes: Use a coarse box grater to shred the parboiled, peeled potatoes into long, thick strands.
  2. Season: Gently toss the potato shreds with salt in a large bowl, being careful not to mash them.
  3. Heat the Pan: Melt half of the clarified butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat until shimmering.
  4. Form the Cake: Add the potatoes to the pan, leveling them out with a spatula. Press down lightly to form a compact disc.
  5. Initial Fry: Cook for 12–15 minutes without stirring. Occasionally shake the pan to ensure it isn't sticking.
  6. The Flip: Slide the Rösti onto a large flat plate. Place the pan upside down over the plate and flip quickly.
  7. Final Crisp: Add remaining butter and sprinkle water into the sides to create steam. Fry for 10 minutes.
  8. Serve: Slide onto a board, garnish with chives, and slice into wedges.
